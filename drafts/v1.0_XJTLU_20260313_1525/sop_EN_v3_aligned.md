## 1. Introduction: The Genesis of My Research Interest

My fascination with gallium nitride (GaN) power devices began during my MSc studies at the University of Bristol, where I first encountered these remarkable wide bandgap semiconductors through coursework in RF engineering and VLSI design. What started as academic curiosity transformed into genuine passion when I conducted COMSOL simulations to optimize GaN HEMT parameters for high-frequency applications. Witnessing how subtle adjustments in device geometry could dramatically enhance power efficiency, I recognized GaN's transformative potential for next-generation power electronics.

This realization was reinforced through my professional experience in the semiconductor industry, where I have spent over three years designing high-voltage analog and power integrated circuits. Working extensively with BCD processes and developing audio power amplifiers and motor driver chips, I have developed deep practical expertise in high-voltage driver design, protection circuit implementation, and power efficiency optimization. These experiences have solidified my commitment to pursuing doctoral research in high-performance GaN power devices and their circuit integration.

## 3. Research and Professional Experience

**THz Receiver LNA Design and GaN HEMT Optimization (University of Bristol, 2021-2022)**

As a Research Assistant at the Quantum Engineering Technology Labs, I led a project designing low-noise amplifiers for terahertz receivers while optimizing GaN HEMT device performance. I employed COMSOL Multiphysics to model and optimize GaN HEMT parameters and constructed a functional THz receiver hardware prototype. This experience provided me with hands-on expertise in RF circuit design, semiconductor device simulation, and experimental validation.

**Audio CLASS-D Power Amplifier Chip Design (LINK-IC CO., Ltd., 2024-Present)**

As an Analog IC Design Engineer at Shanghai LINK-IC Semiconductor, I have been leading the development of consumer-grade audio Class-D power amplifier chips using SMIC's 18nm BCD process. I was responsible for complete chip architecture design and analog circuit implementation. The technical challenges included implementing robust over-current/over-voltage/over-temperature (OC/OV/OT) protection circuits for high-voltage operation. This work directly informs my interest in GaN power device integration, as design challenges in high-voltage isolation, thermal management, and efficiency optimization are directly transferable to GaN-based systems.

**High-Voltage SIP Chip Development (CHIPON, 2023-2024)**

At Shanghai Chipon Microelectronics, I served as Technology Development Engineer and Project Leader for a system-in-package chip development project targeting automotive applications. I led the full development cycle of a high-voltage peripheral sub-chip integrating gate driver, power management unit, programmable gain amplifier, and LIN/CAN communication interfaces. I developed comprehensive expertise in high-voltage protection circuit design and gained hands-on experience with BLDC motor drive modeling. The integration challenges provide a strong foundation for approaching GaN power IC integration.

## 4. Why XJTLU

**Research Alignment with Professor Wen Liu:** Professor Liu's research portfolio in GaN power devices represents an ideal match for my expertise. Her NSFC-funded project "High Performance Gallium Nitride Power Integrated Intelligent Chips" (2023-2027) focuses on developing high-performance GaN driver integrated circuits. My proposed research will contribute by addressing six key areas: (1) integrated OC/OV/OT protection circuits, (2) high dV/dt immunity level shifting, (3) precision current sensing, (4) thermal-electrical co-design, (5) dynamic reliability enhancement, and (6) AI-assisted scripting tools for automated EKV model extraction. This alignment leverages my background in high-voltage driver design and protection circuits.

**Proven Research Environment:** Professor Liu has secured over 8 million RMB in funding as Principal Investigator, including grants from the National Natural Science Foundation and National Key R&D Programme. This ensures access to state-of-the-art facilities including XJTLU's in-house 2μm GaN facility and external foundry partnerships. Her publication record—50+ papers in IEEE EDL, TED, and TPE—demonstrates high academic standards.

**International Academic Culture:** XJTLU's position as a Sino-foreign cooperative university awarding degrees from the University of Liverpool creates an international research environment building upon my UK education experience.

**Industry Integration:** Partnerships with companies including Huawei provide pathways for translating research into commercial applications, essential for meeting stringent reliability requirements in automotive and industrial markets.

## 5. Future Study Plans

I have structured my doctoral research as a **4-year programme** with **four tape-out iterations**:

**Year 1 (Months 1-12):** Complete coursework and literature review. Analyze PDKs for 0.25μm, 0.5μm, and 2μm GaN processes. Design circuit architectures for six research questions.

**Year 2 (Months 13-24):** **Tape-out #1** for individual circuit block validation. Develop AI-assisted scripting tools for automated EKV model extraction (RQ6).

**Year 3 (Months 25-36):** **Tape-out #2** for full-system integration. **Tape-out #3** for multi-process validation across 0.25μm, 0.5μm, and 2μm variants.

**Year 4 (Months 37-48):** **Tape-out #4** as final system demonstration. Complete dissertation and target 2-3 first-author publications in IEEE journals (EDL, TED, or TPE).
