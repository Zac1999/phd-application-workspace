## 1. Introduction: The Genesis of My Research Interest

My fascination with gallium nitride (GaN) power devices began during my MSc studies at the University of Bristol, where I first encountered these remarkable wide bandgap semiconductors through coursework in RF engineering and VLSI design. What started as academic curiosity quickly transformed into genuine passion when I conducted COMSOL simulations to optimize GaN HEMT parameters for high-frequency communication applications. Witnessing firsthand how subtle adjustments in device geometry could dramatically enhance power efficiency and switching performance, I recognized GaN's transformative potential for next-generation power electronics. This realization, combined with subsequent industry experience designing GaN driver integrated circuits, has solidified my commitment to pursuing doctoral research in this field. I am now eager to join XJTLU under Professor Wen Liu's supervision to contribute to advancing high-performance GaN power devices and their circuit integration.

## 3. Research Experience

**THz Receiver LNA Design and GaN HEMT Optimization (University of Bristol, 2021-2022)**

As a Research Assistant at the Quantum Engineering Technology Labs, I led a project designing low-noise amplifiers (LNA) for terahertz receivers while optimizing GaN HEMT device performance. My responsibilities encompassed three critical phases: first, I independently designed and simulated the LNA circuit architecture; second, I employed COMSOL Multiphysics to model and optimize GaN HEMT parameters, resulting in enhanced power efficiency for wireless transceiver applications; third, I constructed a functional THz receiver hardware prototype that established the foundation for high-speed GaN communication devices. This experience provided me with hands-on expertise in RF circuit design, semiconductor device simulation, and experimental validation—skills that remain directly relevant to my proposed doctoral research.

**Basic Analog IP Design for GaN Driver Modules (HIT, 2020-2021)**

At HIT's Microelectronics Research Center, I designed fundamental analog building blocks for GaN driver applications. I independently developed a single-stage folded cascode operational amplifier and bandgap reference circuits, then completed the full design flow for a 5V output LDO—from schematic capture through layout implementation to post-layout simulation verification. This project cultivated my proficiency in analog IC design using Cadence tools and gave me practical experience with the complete IC development cycle, from concept to tape-out preparation.
