## 3. Research and Professional Experience

**THz Receiver LNA Design and GaN HEMT Optimization (University of Bristol, 2021-2022)**

As a Research Assistant at the Quantum Engineering Technology Labs, I led a project designing low-noise amplifiers (LNA) for terahertz receivers while optimizing GaN HEMT device performance. This experience provided me with hands-on expertise in RF circuit design, semiconductor device simulation, and experimental validation—skills that remain directly relevant to my proposed doctoral research.

**Audio CLASS-D Power Amplifier Chip Design (LINK-IC CO., Ltd., 2024-Present)**

As an Analog IC Design Engineer at Shanghai LINK-IC Semiconductor, I have been leading the development of consumer-grade audio Class-D power amplifier chips using SMIC's 18nm BCD process. This project represents a significant advancement in my expertise with high-voltage power IC design. I was responsible for complete chip architecture design, analog circuit implementation, and collaboration with automotive customers to optimize functionality for wireless audio applications. Two design solutions have been successfully deployed in commercial wireless audio devices.

The technical challenges of this project were substantial: achieving high-efficiency power conversion while maintaining audio fidelity, implementing robust over-current/over-voltage/over-temperature (OC/OV/OT) protection circuits for high-voltage operation, and optimizing electromagnetic compatibility for consumer electronics. Working with the 18nm BCD process provided invaluable experience in advanced power device integration, where high-voltage DMOS transistors coexist with precision analog and digital control circuits. This work directly informs my interest in GaN power device integration, as many of the design challenges—high-voltage isolation, thermal management, and efficiency optimization—are directly transferable to GaN-based systems.

**High-Voltage SIP Chip Development (CHIPON, 2023-2024)**

During my tenure at Shanghai Chipon Microelectronics, I served as Technology Development Engineer and Project Leader for a system-in-package (SIP) chip development project targeting automotive applications. I led the full development cycle of a high-voltage peripheral sub-chip integrating gate driver (DRIVER), power management unit (PMU), programmable gain amplifier (PGA), and LIN/CAN communication interfaces.

This experience was particularly valuable for understanding the complexities of high-voltage integrated circuit design in harsh automotive environments. I developed comprehensive expertise in high-voltage protection circuit design, including over-current detection, over-voltage clamping, and thermal shutdown mechanisms. The project required extensive collaboration with process engineers to optimize device characteristics for high-voltage operation while maintaining reliability under automotive qualification standards (AEC-Q100). Additionally, I gained hands-on experience with BLDC motor drive modeling using SVPWM and BEMF control algorithms, which deepened my understanding of power electronics system-level design.

The integration challenges encountered in this SIP development—managing high-voltage and low-voltage domains, minimizing parasitic effects, and ensuring robust protection under fault conditions—provide a strong foundation for approaching GaN power IC integration challenges. The experience of coordinating cross-functional teams and managing full chip development cycles from specification to tape-out has also prepared me for the collaborative and systematic nature of doctoral research.
